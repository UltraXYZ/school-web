Campus Life

The Playground

The playground has recently been rebuilt and upgraded with funding from the PTA. It has multiple slides and places where you can play during recess. There is a jungle gym, monkey bars, and some swings. Many students enjoy playing there, and even go there after school to play and have fun with their friends. We hope that if your child comes over to CRS, he/she enjoys our newly updated playground as well! (The picture is of the old playground.)

The gymnasium/stage

The gym/stage area is where we do PE, the Bobcat Fitness Club, and so much more! We use the stage for performances and assemblies, while the gym is mainly used for PE. CRS's gymnasium is massive, with 4 basketball hoops, a rock climbing wall, and all sorts of equipment in the storage room. The stage is also pretty big, and we have had many assemblies there, with the performers/presenters on the stage, while the students are sitting on the gym floor.

The Cafeteria

The cafeteria is where the students gather to eat lunch during school hours. They can also eat breakfast there, if they arrive early enough. There is a specified lunch menu for every day. Cookie Day is normally Thursday, while Pizza Day is always Friday. There are a variety of meals, and drinks are provided. (Choices of milk (plain, chocolate, or strawberry (occasionally)) or water. (8 or 16 oz. bottles)) You can also pack your own lunch and bring it to school in a lunch box.

School Achievements

Our AAP (Advanced Academics Program) Program
Critical and Creative Thinking Lessons

Level 1 (Critical and Creative Thinking Lessons) is provided to students grades K-6, and consists of model lessons that are designed to teach critical and creative thinking skills. The lessons are modeled in all classrooms by Advanced Academic Resource Teachers (AARTs) and the thinking strategies are then used throughout the year by classroom and other teachers who work with students. Student responses to these lessons are used as part of the identification process for Levels II-IV. Parents may also practice these strategies during family conversations and activities. In Level 2 (Differentiated Lessons in Areas of Academic Strength, Grades ), the AART collaborates with classroom teachers to provide additional challenges through lessons and resources that extend and enrich the Program of Studies. Screening for this level of service is ongoing throughout the school year. For Level 3 (Part-Time Advanced Academic Programs), students identified by our local Advanced Academic committee work directly with the AART each week in a small group setting.

The PPS (Prize Point System)
The point system

We understand that being a great role model for kids is very hard. Instead in our school we do a point system in which when a child does a good deed he/she gets a point. If a child helped open the door: he would get 1 point. If a child help the teacher after school to clean the classroom they get 3 points etc. The greater the deed the more points. They can exchange points for prizes such as 5 points gives you a pencil, 30 points give you a pencil case, 90 points for a note book, and our biggest prize is for 200 points = a king sized chocolate bar. We change our prizes every month so students don't get bored of the same prizes.
